Did You Know?
The Filipino Value of "Hiya"
Filipinos have a strong sense of "hiya" (sense of shame/propriety). Avoiding public embarrassment — for yourself and others — is key to respectful interaction.
✅ Do's
❌ Don'ts
🙏
✅ Do
Use "po" and "opo" when speaking to elders
These words show deep respect in Filipino culture and are expected when talking to older people.
👟
❌ Don't
Wear shoes inside someone's home
Always remove footwear before entering a Filipino household — look for shoes by the door as your cue.
🤝
✅ Do
Accept food or drinks when offered by a host
Refusing hospitality can be seen as rude. Even a small taste is appreciated and respectful.
😤
❌ Don't
Raise your voice or show anger in public
Filipinos value keeping the peace ("pakikisama"). Loud confrontations cause shame for everyone involved.
🫱
✅ Do
Use your lips or eyes to point a direction
Pointing with a finger is considered rude. Filipinos often point with their lips or a subtle nod — try it!
📸
❌ Don't
Take photos inside churches without asking
Many churches prohibit photography during Mass. Always check for signs and ask permission first.
